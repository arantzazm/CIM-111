About Me

Jane

I came out when I was 14 as bisexual to my mom. I wrote it in a letter explaining who I was as a person and that my sexuality didn’t define me. She never read it, she ripped it up and told me “I already know”. She then hit me in my face, and told me “I didn’t raise you to be like this”. Coming out the first time was the hardest. The second time I was outed as a lesbian by people in a small town I used to live in, I was 17. My mom finally accepted me and our relationship grew stronger! What the LGBTQ+ community means to me is full of pride, love, acceptance and of course, diversity. We are all our own person-we’re all unique and that’s what really makes the LGBTQ+ community THE community. Love is love, don’t ever let anyone tell you different!
